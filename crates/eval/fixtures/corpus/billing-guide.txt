Northwind Supplies — Billing and Invoicing Guide

Plain text on purpose: the corpus must not be all markdown, because the parser treats .txt and .md
identically today and a structure-aware chunker would not.

INVOICES

Invoices are issued on the first working day of the month for the preceding month. Each invoice
carries a reference in the format INV followed by the year and a five digit sequence, for example
INV-2024-00317. An invoice cannot be reissued with a different billing address once it has been
finalised; a credit note is issued instead.

PAYMENT TERMS

Consumer orders are charged at dispatch, not at the time of ordering. Business accounts are invoiced
monthly with 30 day payment terms. Late payment carries interest at 8 percent above the base rate,
applied from the day after the due date.

VAT

Prices shown to consumers include VAT at the destination country's rate. Business customers with a
valid VAT number registered in the account are invoiced without VAT under the reverse charge
mechanism. A VAT number added after an invoice is finalised does not retroactively change it.

PURCHASE ORDERS

Business accounts may quote a purchase order number, which is printed on the invoice. We do not
require a purchase order and will not block a dispatch waiting for one.

DISPUTES

A billing dispute must be raised within 60 days of the invoice date. Disputed amounts are not subject
to late payment interest while an investigation is open.
